Importance of supporting Waste Management Efforts:

Supporting waste management efforts is crucial for a healthy community as proper waste management reduces pollution, conserves natural resources, and helps in mitigating the impacts of climate change. By supporting these efforts we play a vital role in protecting our environment and ensuring a better quality of life for current and future generations. We thank you for your commitment to such a noble cause!

Saahas, founded in 2001 by former journalist Wilma Rodrigues, is a non-profit registered under the Karnataka Societies Registration Act 1960. Established to promote waste management practices in line with the Municipal Solid Wastes (Management and Handling) Rules, 2000, Saahas focuses on resource recovery. The name 'Saahas,' meaning courage, reflects the bold step taken to address solid waste management.
In 2008, British traveler Jodie Underhill visited India and, struck by its beauty but dismayed by its garbage problem, founded the voluntary organization 'Mountain Cleaners.' She organized weekly cleanups at Triund, a remote mountain camp near McLeod Ganj. In 2012, Jodie registered the non-profit Waste Warriors in Dehradun, aiming to clean up the entire country. Early leaders Tashi Pareek and Minakshi Pandey joined her efforts, focusing on Dharamshala, Dehradun, and the outskirts of the Corbett Tiger Reserve.
WasteAid.org is a non-profit organization dedicated to improving waste management practices in low-income countries. Their mission is to create lasting environmental and social impacts by turning waste into useful resources, thus promoting sustainability and community well-being.
